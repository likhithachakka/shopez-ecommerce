## Purpose

An empathy map is a simple, easy-to-digest visual that captures knowledge about a user's behaviours and attitudes. It helps teams better understand their users by considering things from the user's perspective along with his or her goals and challenges.

**Primary Persona:** An online shopper (ages 18–45) who values convenience, variety, and a frictionless purchase experience.

---

## Empathy Map Canvas

| Dimension | What the user experiences |
|---|---|
| **Think & Feel** | "I want quality products without spending hours browsing." / "I hope checkout is quick and secure." / "Will my order arrive on time?" / "I wonder if there are hidden fees at checkout." |
| **Hear** | Friends talking about great deals online. Ads for trendy products on social media. Reviews from other buyers about product quality and delivery. News about data breaches on shopping sites. |
| **See** | Cluttered e-commerce sites with slow loading times. Unclear return policies hidden in fine print. Limited payment options. Pop-up ads and irrelevant recommendations. Complex navigation menus. |
| **Say & Do** | Searches for specific product categories using search bar. Compares prices across multiple products before deciding. Adds items to cart but hesitates before checking out. Abandons cart when sign-up is required or checkout takes too long. Reads reviews before purchasing. |
| **Pains** | Too many options leading to decision fatigue and choice paralysis. Hidden costs revealed only at checkout (shipping, taxes, surcharges). Unreliable delivery estimates — orders arrive late or not at all. Complex, multi-step return processes. Forced account creation before browsing or purchasing. Slow website performance on mobile data. |
| **Gains** | Curated product lists that reduce browsing time. Simple, fast checkout with minimal steps. Transparent pricing with discount visibility upfront. Order tracking so they know exactly when items will arrive. Guest checkout with no account required. Clean, fast-loading interface across all devices. |

---

## Key Insights

| Insight | Implication for ShopEZ |
|---|---|
| Users want **fewer, better choices** not infinite selection | Curated catalogue — each category shows a hand-picked selection with item counts |
| Users **abandon carts** when checkout is long or requires sign-up | Guest-first, one-page checkout with no account gate |
| Users **distrust hidden costs** | Every product card shows full ₹ price + discount badge — no surprises |
| Users need **post-purchase confidence** | Order confirmation immediately after placement + full order history |
| Users browse on **mobile + variable connectivity** | Fast-loading SPA with fallback data ensures the UI is never empty |
